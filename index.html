Yoga of Sri Chinmoy

According to Sri Chinmoy, the word yoga is derived from the Sanskrit for “plow,” which is also the source for the word “yoke” — which joins the oxen and the plow. From the spiritual point of view, Sri Chinmoy says that yoga means “union with God.” In Indian philosophy — the philosophy of spirituality and mysticism — we learn that there are three main paths that lead to God-realisation: Bhakti Yoga, the path of love and devotion; Jnana Yoga, the path of knowledge; and Karma Yoga, the path of action. Sri Chinmoy’s yoga philosophy combines these three different forms to create an integral approach to God that is uniquely suited to the Western world. He calls his path “the sunlit path of love, devotion and surrender.”

Sri Chinmoy

Sri Chinmoy is a spiritual teacher, or guru, who has expressed his philosophy in some 1,500 books. These books contain essays, answers to questions posed by seekers, lyrical poems, memorable sayings, illumining stories drawn from his personal experiences and plays about the lives of the great spiritual Masters. His language speaks directly to the heart of his readers. He has a unique ability to convey abstract philosophical concepts simply, powerfully and meaningfully — and this is an aspect of Sri Chinmoy and his teachings that appeals greatly to seekers.

The Higher Worlds

One of Sri Chinmoy’s unique abilities is to be able to meditate deeply and enter the higher worlds at will. In his writings — many of which are included in the yogaofsrichinmoy site, Sri Chinmoy describes in detail many of the higher experiences, such as nirvana, samadhi and the vision of the Supreme. Sri Chinmoy’s life work has been to share with others the free access to the same source that he has discovered through his life of meditation and prayer. Among the many capacities that Sri Chinmoy describes are intuitive power, the gift of creativity, continuous inspiration and abiding joy. Sri Chinmoy says that all these become readily available to the seeker who can go beyond the superficial concerns of the mind and enter into the higher worlds.

Transforming the Earth Plane

Sri Chinmoy believes that the earth will be transformed into the kingdom of heaven in the fulness of time. He says that the processes of reincarnation and evolution are linked and result in the steady transformation of each individual and the perfection of life on earth. Sri Chinmoy believes that when there is perfect harmony between the inner life and the outer life, this earth plane will be inundated with the qualities of the higher worlds: wisdom, peace, light, bliss and love. Sri Chinmoy also says that men and women should go together in their spiritual quest.
